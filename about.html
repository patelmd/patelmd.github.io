More About Me

Six months ago, I decided to change my career from being an entrepreneur. After some serious self-reflection about my passions and interests, I committed to learning everything I could about machine learning, data science, and the tech industry. I committed between 4-6 hours each day to learn the basics and to regain a strong foothold as a full-time data scientist.

Today, I have published projects with the skills and methodologies I have learned over the past few months. My strength lies in recognizing patterns, correlations in data, and creatively tell the story of my data analysis.

However, I still have a long way to go. I further hope to get real life experience so I can engage with other like-minded people and get more experience building intelligent systems for use in the real-world and to help companies make smart data-driven decisions.

Any Questions?

Why did you make this website?
I created and deployed this website because I wanted to showcase my projects and the works I have done through my portfolio. I want to show the creative story-telling side of me. The only way to do that was to make a website and let my portfolio speaks for itself. Though I don't expect it to be anything more than a portfolio site, I strongly suspect that these skills will be crucial to technological development in the years to come.

How long have you been practicing data science and machine learning?

I discovered data science and machine learning a year ago after researching of how to use data and analytics to drive more customers into my pharmacy business. Since then I have immersed myself in data science communities on Towards Data Science on Medium and across the web.

I have pushed myself to not only understand the theory behind data science and machine learning, but also the constraints from producing datasets to help influence business decisions.

Do you have any experience with database management?

I have knowledges of SQL queries and have had experience working with PostgreSQL and MYSQL.

I believe that data scientists who can own every step of the Machine learning lifecycle will be invaluable in the coming years especially with those with designed thinkings and agile methodologies.

Why are you interested in data science / machine learning?

I am curious by nature. Data science and Machine learning are both excellent tools to understand the world better.

Despite the movement towards integrating AI into existing technologies, the implementation of machine learning is still in its infancy. I want to work with the pioneers and innovators that create new and amazing things.

What industry are you looking to work in?

I am very interested in analytics and data manipulations. I think the best fit for me will be a career in the tech industry.

I am drawn towards the industry's focus on rapid improvement, innovation, and team-oriented creativity.

What kind of work do you see yourself doing?

I am interested in building machine learning applications that can communicate with the back end architectures of existing applications.

I describe my perfect future position to be a mix of data science, machine learning engineering, and data visualization. Essentially a machine learning specialist with the skills to deploy application as well as tell a story creatively and visually.
